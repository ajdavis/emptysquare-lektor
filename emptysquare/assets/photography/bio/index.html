I'm an amateur portrait photographer in New York City, trained at the International Center for Photography.

I've covered events, created editorial portraits, and photographed landscapes for Der Spiegel, New York Magazine, Buddhadharma Magazine, and The Shambhala Sun. In 2012 my work for The Lo-Down NY was awarded an Ippy prize for best photo essay by the CUNY Graduate School of Journalism.

I'm now mainly documenting transitional-housing facilities, the Lower East Side, and Zen Buddhists in New York City.
